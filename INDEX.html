Overview of the Internet

The Internet, sometimes known as the World Wide Web, is a global network that links millions of computers worldwide. It has transformed communication, information exchange, and commerce, becoming an essential component of contemporary civilization.

Its beginnings may be traced back to the late 1960s, when the United States Department of Defense supported the creation of ARPANET, a decentralized communication network that was resistant to nuclear assaults.

The Internet has advanced significantly over the years, including the development of hypertext markup language (HTML), which enabled the production of the first web pages, and the advent of the browser, which made traversing the online more user-friendly.

Key Milestones in Internet History

  • Tim Berners-Lee invented the World Wide Web in 1989.
  • WorldWideWeb, the original web browser, was introduced in 1990.
  • The commercialization of the Internet in the 1990s resulted in the dot-com boom.
  • The introduction of search engines such as Google has revolutionized information retrieval.
  • The rise of social media platforms like Facebook and Twitter has transformed online communication.
